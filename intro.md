# Molecular Transport Phenomena Pre-Course

Welcome to the Jupyter Book for the Molecular Transport Phenomena Pre-Course for MSc Chemical Engineering at TU Delft! This pre-course was designed to ensure every student is ready to take Molecular Transport Phenomena.

This pre-course covers mathematical foundations and reviews BSc-level transport phenomena concepts necessary to start Molecular Transport Phenomena. 


## Purpose of the Pre-Course
The main goal of this chapter is to clarify the expected entry level for this course. It builds on courses in your undergraduate program, particularly on Transport Phenomena, Mathematics (including differential equations and statistical methods), Physical Chemistry, and Thermodynamics. Please note that some of you may have seen, instead of one course on 'Transport Phenomena', a series of separate courses on 'heat transfer', 'mass transfer', and 'fluid flow', which together comprise the area of Transport Phenomena. 

To pre-assess your **prior knowledge for the course**, ensure that you are familiar with these topics and comfortable solving the respective exercises. Completing the exercises helps you to identify the gaps in theoretical knowledge/practical skills, such that you have a chance to brush them up before the course starts.

## Pre-Requisites for MTP
### 1. Mathematics

#### Differential equations

Can you solve differential equations?

* apply the chain rule when differentiating
* solve homogeneous first-order ordinary differential equations
* solve non-homogeneous first-order ordinary differential equations
* solve homogeneous second-order ordinary differential equations
* simplify a differential equation or its solution using Taylor expansions (and use Taylor and series expansions in a more general context)

#### Integration

Can you solve integrals?

* integrate basic functions, for example using a relevant substitution
* or using integration by parts 

#### Statistics

Can you do the following?

* recall the law of large numbers
* recall the formula that describes a Gaussian distribution
* recall the general formulas to calculate the mean and the mean
  square value of a quantity that is distributed according to a given distribution function; recall the formula to calculate the standard deviation based on the mean and mean square values; apply the
  formulas to a given problem 

### 2. Transport Phenomena

Can you explain the following concepts?

* steady state, quasi steady state, transient
* batch, plug flow, and stirred tank reactor
* Poiseuille flow & incompressible flow 
* dimensionless number & Reynolds number 
* inertial force & drag force 
* no slip & no shear boundary condition
* zero flux & continuity of flux boundary condition
* continuum approximation 
* Knudsen number
* diffusive transport &  convective transport in the context of mass, heat, and momentum transport
* Peclet number
* penetration theory



Can you recall the following laws and equations?  
* Newton's second law; Fick's second law of diffusion
* Fick's first law of diffusion and Fourier's law of conduction
* Hagen-Poisseuille equation for steady laminar flow through a cylindrical pipe

Are you able to perform the following analysis? 
* analyze problems involving transport of mass, momentum, and energy in
  Cartesian, cylindrical, and spherical coordinates by:
    * choosing a relevant control volume
    * indicating the fluxes entering and leaving the control volume
    * setting up a material balance, species balance, momentum balance, or
    energy balance
    * simplifying the balance equation into a partial differential
    equation that captures how the parameter of interest (e.g. mass,
    concentration, temperature, velocity) depends on space and time
    * formulating the boundary and initial conditions for the differential
    equation
    * solving the differential equation

* use the above strategy to derive the 'Hagen-Poisseuille equation' 
* explain the physical meaning of the different terms in the
  Navier-Stokes equations and able to simplify the Navier-Stokes
  equations when analyzing a particular problem 
* analyze problems involving forces, by setting up a force balance
  starting from Newton's second law of physics 
* apply penetration theory to analyse (non-steady state) problems involving diffusion 

### 3. Physical Chemistry
Can you explain and apply these concepts?
* explain the concept of mean free path
* explain the concept of equi-partition
* explain the background of Maxwell-Boltzmann distributions
* work with the ideal gas law
